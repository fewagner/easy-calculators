2. Derivation of the mutual capacitance

2.1 Strategy. No closed-form exact solution exists for the mutual capacitance between a circular island and a CPW strip with surrounding ground plane. The formula below is a geometric view-factor approximation that integrates an angle-dependent coupling kernel over the effective feedline width. It captures all correct parametric trends and is accurate to a factor of 2вҖ“3 versus full EM simulation.

2.2 Effective parameters. Charge in a CPW structure distributes across the gap, not only at the conductor edge. Each conductor+gap pair is replaced by an effective size at the midpoint between the conductor edge and the gap edge:

\[R_\text{eff} = \frac{d}{2} + \frac{s}{4}, \qquad W_\text{eff} = \frac{w}{2} + \frac{a}{4}, \qquad D = \frac{d}{2} + \text{gap} + \frac{w}{2}\]

2.3 View-factor integral. Consider a point at distance \(x\) from the island centre along the feedline axis. The half-angle subtended by the effective island circle from that point is \(\theta(x) = \arcsin(R_\text{eff}/x)\). We use the coupling kernel \(\sec\theta - 1 = x/\sqrt{x^2 - R_\text{eff}^2} - 1\), which vanishes as \(x\to\infty\) and diverges as \(x\to R_\text{eff}\). Integrating across the effective feedline width \([D - W_\text{eff},\; D + W_\text{eff}]\):

\[C_\text{mf} = 4\,\varepsilon_\text{eff}\,\varepsilon_0 \int_{D-W_\text{eff}}^{D+W_\text{eff}} \!\!\left[\frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2 - R_\text{eff}^2}} - 1\right] dx\]

2.4 Closed-form result. Using the antiderivative \(\int\!\left[x/\sqrt{x^2-R^2}-1\right]dx = \sqrt{x^2-R^2}-x+\text{const}\):

\[C_\text{mf} \approx 4\,\varepsilon_\text{eff}\,\varepsilon_0 \Bigl[\sqrt{(D+W_\text{eff})^2 - R_\text{eff}^2} - \sqrt{(D-W_\text{eff})^2 - R_\text{eff}^2} - 2\,W_\text{eff}\Bigr]\]

Validity condition:

\[D - W_\text{eff} > R_\text{eff} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \text{gap} > \frac{s}{4} + \frac{a}{4} \quad(\text{typically a few }\mu\text{m})\]

2.5 Large-distance limit. Expanding for \(D \gg W_\text{eff}, R_\text{eff}\):

\[C_\text{mf} \;\longrightarrow\; \frac{4\,\varepsilon_\text{eff}\,\varepsilon_0\,R_\text{eff}^2\,W_\text{eff}}{D^2} \qquad(1/D^2 \text{ dipole-like decay})\]

At the nominal geometry \(D/R_\text{eff} \approx 5\), the full formula is needed вҖ” the island body contributes significantly and cannot be treated as a point dipole.

3. From C_mf to TвӮҒ

Modelling the qubit as an LC oscillator with total capacitance \(C_\Sigma\), the admittance seen through \(C_\text{mf}\) into a matched feedline (impedance \(Z_0\)) is \(Y(\omega) = j\omega C_\text{mf}/(1 + j\omega C_\text{mf}Z_0)\). Since \(\omega C_\text{mf}Z_0 \sim 10^{-4} \ll 1\):

\[\operatorname{Re}[Y(\omega)] \approx \omega^2 C_\text{mf}^2\,Z_0\]

An infinite feedline has two ports вҖ” the factor of 2 is essential and often omitted in the literature:

\[\kappa = \frac{2\,\omega^2 C_\text{mf}^2\,Z_0}{C_\Sigma}, \qquad T_1 = \frac{C_\Sigma}{2\,\omega^2 C_\text{mf}^2\,Z_0}\]

This follows from input-output theory (Blais et al., Rev. Mod. Phys. 93, 025005, 2021; Koch et al., Phys. Rev. A 76, 042319, 2007). \(C_\text{mf}\) is the mutual capacitance computed above; \(C_\Sigma\) is the total qubit self-capacitance, estimated separately вҖ” typically 40вҖ“100 fF for a 125 Вөm transmon island.
