Qubit–Feedline T1 Estimator

Analytic estimate of the T1 relaxation time of a circular transmon qubit island due to capacitive coupling to a nearby CPW feedline — comparing two models: Model A (no ground plane, monopole coupling) and Model B (coplanar ground plane, dipole-screened coupling). For design guidance only — always verify with EM simulation (Sonnet, HFSS, Palace).

Formulas Used

Shared Definitions

ε_eff = (1 + ε_Si) / 2 [effective medium: vacuum above, silicon below] D = r + d + w/2 [center-to-center distance, m] ω = 2π · f_q [qubit angular frequency, rad/s]

Model A — No Ground Plane (monopole / flux-interception)

C_self = 8 · ε_eff · ε₀ · r [exact result for conducting disk in uniform medium; Jackson, Classical Electrodynamics, 3rd ed., prob. 3.3] η = arctan(w / (2D)) / π [solid-angle fraction subtended by infinite strip; from van Oosterom & Strackee rect. formula, limit length→∞] C_qf = C_self · η [flux interception; requires d/r ≫ 1] κ = 2 · ω² · C_qf² · Z₀ / C_Σ [decay rate; factor 2 = both feedline ports; valid when ω·C_qf·Z₀ ≪ 1] T1 = 1/κ = C_Σ / (2·ω²·C_qf²·Z₀) [Blais et al., Rev. Mod. Phys. 93, 025005 (2021); Koch et al., Phys. Rev. A 76, 042319 (2007)]

Model B — Coplanar Ground Plane (dipole / screened)

Physical picture: Ground plane carries induced charge -Q on its inner face. Net charge of (island + ground) = 0 → looks like a dipole (p ~ Q·s) from far away. Far field ∝ 1/r³ instead of 1/r² → coupling to feedline is strongly suppressed. C_qf,scr = ε_eff · ε₀ · s_island · s_feedline / D [general form] = ε_eff · ε₀ · s² / D [if both CPW gaps equal s] [requires s ≪ D] [Jackson, Classical Electrodynamics, §2.9] T1,scr = C_Σ / (2·ω²·C_qf,scr²·Z₀) Improvement factor = T1,scr / T1,A = (C_qf,A / C_qf,scr)²

Assumptions & Limitations

Assumption When it breaks down Expected error
A d/r ≫ 1
Island = point monopole		 d/r < 5; default geometry has d/r ≈ 4 20–40% overestimate of Cqf at d/r ≈ 4. Unreliable below d/r = 2.
B s ≪ D
Dipole approximation		 s/D > 0.10; quadrupole terms grow Model overestimates T1 when s is not small vs D. Use EM simulation for large s.
A+B εeff = (1 + εSi)/2 Thick substrate, surface effects, crystal anisotropy ~5–10% error on all capacitances
A+B Perfect circular disk Non-circular pads, dumbbell or cross shapes ~10–20% deviation in Cself
A+B Feedline matched at Z0, no resonance Impedance mismatch or standing wave near fq Can increase effective coupling by orders of magnitude — not captured here
A+B No Purcell filter A Purcell filter is present in the readout chain T1 enhanced by (Δ/g)², typically 100–1000×. This tool gives the worst-case unfiltered estimate.
A+B No readout resonator Qubit dispersively coupled to a resonator Adds a separate Purcell decay channel; estimate independently.
B sisland = sfeedline = s Different CPW gaps around island vs feedline Replace s² with sisland · sfeedline in the formula.
A+B Factor of 2 (both ports) Feedline terminated or one-sided Use factor 1 for a one-sided feedline.
A+B Overall analytic accuracy All regimes Factor ~2–3 on Cqf vs. full EM simulation. Always verify critical designs with Sonnet, HFSS, or Palace/ElmerFEM.

Recommended workflow:
1. Use this tool to check that the geometry is not obviously wrong.
2. If the estimated T1 is within a factor of 5 of the target, run a full EM simulation.
3. Extract Cqf from the simulation and use the same T1 formula for a reliable number.
