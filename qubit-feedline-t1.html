Qubit–Feedline T₁ Estimator

Analytic estimate of the T₁ relaxation time of a circular transmon qubit island due to capacitive coupling to a CPW feedline, using a view-factor integral over effective geometric parameters. Geometry: fully planar chip with a continuous ground plane and etched cutouts around the island and feedline. Accuracy ~2–3× vs. full EM simulation — always verify with Sonnet, HFSS, or Palace.

2. Derivation of the mutual capacitance

2.1 Strategy. No closed-form exact solution exists for the mutual capacitance between a circular island and a CPW strip with surrounding ground plane. The formula below is a geometric view-factor approximation that integrates an angle-dependent coupling kernel over the effective feedline width. It captures all correct parametric trends and is accurate to a factor of 2–3 versus full EM simulation.

2.2 Effective parameters. Charge in a CPW structure distributes across the gap, not only at the conductor edge. Each conductor+gap pair is replaced by an effective size at the midpoint between the conductor edge and the gap edge:

R_eff = d/2 + s/4 (effective radius: island + half the island gap) W_eff = w/2 + a/4 (effective half-width: feedline + half the CPW gap) D = d/2 + gap + w/2 (center-to-center distance)

2.3 View-factor integral. Consider a point at distance x from the island center along the feedline axis. The half-angle subtended by the effective island circle from that point is θ(x) = arcsin(R_eff/x). We use the coupling kernel sec θ − 1 = x/√(x²−R_eff²) − 1, which vanishes as x→∞ and diverges as x→R_eff (contact). Integrating across the effective feedline width [D−W_eff, D+W_eff]:

C_mf = 4 ε_eff ε₀ · ∫[D−W_eff to D+W_eff] [ x/√(x²−R_eff²) − 1 ] dx

2.4 Closed-form result. Using the antiderivative ∫[x/√(x²−R²) − 1] dx = √(x²−R²) − x + const:

C_mf ≈ 4 ε_eff ε₀ · [ √((D+W_eff)²−R_eff²) − √((D−W_eff)²−R_eff²) − 2 W_eff ] Validity condition: D − W_eff > R_eff ⟺ gap > s/4 + a/4 (typically a few µm)

2.5 Large-distance limit. Expanding for D ≫ W_eff, R_eff:

C_mf → 4 ε_eff ε₀ R_eff² W_eff / D² (1/D² dipole-like decay) At nominal geometry D/R_eff ≈ 5, so the full formula is needed — the island body contributes significantly and cannot be treated as a point dipole.
3. From C_mf to T₁

Modelling the qubit as an LC oscillator with total capacitance C_Σ, the admittance seen through C_mf into a matched feedline (impedance Z₀) is Y(ω) = jωC_mf / (1 + jωC_mf Z₀). Since ωC_mf Z₀ ~ 10⁻⁴ ≪ 1:

Re[Y(ω)] ≈ ω² C_mf² Z₀

An infinite feedline has two ports — the factor of 2 below is essential and often omitted in the literature:

κ = 2 ω² C_mf² Z₀ / C_Σ T₁ = C_Σ / (2 ω² C_mf² Z₀)

This follows from input-output theory (Blais et al., Rev. Mod. Phys. 93, 025005, 2021; Koch et al., Phys. Rev. A 76, 042319, 2007). C_mf is the mutual capacitance computed above. C_Σ is the total qubit self-capacitance, estimated separately — typically 40–100 fF for a 125 µm transmon island.

Assumptions & limitations
#Assumption / LimitationExpected impact
1Planar 2D geometry. All conductors coplanar on one metal layer.Appropriate for planar CPW chips. Small error (<10%).
2Uniform ε_eff = (1+ε_Si)/2 = 6.35. Treats the Si/vacuum interface as a uniform effective medium.5–10% on all capacitances. Depends on substrate thickness and conductor width.
3Coupling kernel sec θ − 1 is a geometric approximation, not a rigorous EM solution.Factor 2–3 vs. full EM simulation (Sonnet, HFSS). Best available closed form.
4Effective parameters R_eff = d/2 + s/4 and W_eff = w/2 + a/4 place the effective boundary at the midpoint between conductor edge and gap edge.~20% on C_mf. Other weightings are plausible; midpoint minimises known error.
5Formula is specific to the with-ground-plane geometry.Does not apply to geometries without a coplanar ground plane.
6Feedline is infinite and perfectly impedance-matched at Z₀.Negligible if terminated properly. A resonance near f_q can dramatically increase coupling.
7Both feedline ports counted (factor of 2 in κ). For a one-sided feedline, remove the factor of 2.Factor of 2 error in κ and T₁ if wrong.
8No Purcell filter or coupling resonator. This is the direct radiation T₁ limit.With a Purcell filter, T₁ improves by (Δ/g)², typically 100–1000×.
9C_Σ (total qubit capacitance) is a free parameter, not computed from geometry.Uncertainty in C_Σ propagates linearly to T₁.
10JJ leads not modelled. If leads point toward the feedline, C_mf increases locally.Check layout — JJ leads should point away from the feedline.
11Only feedline coupling modelled. TLS dielectric loss, quasiparticle loss, and coupling to other transmission lines are not included.Can be significant — this calculator gives only the feedline T₁ limit.
