Qubit–Feedline T₁ Estimator

Cmf and Cgnd are read from a BEM lookup table and linearly interpolated. T₁ is then calculated with input-output theory.

Method
Boundary-element method (BEM), Maxwell capacitance matrix
Substrate
Half silicon / half vacuum — εeff = (1 + εSi)/2 = 6.35
Feedline
Length 1.98 mm, centre-conductor width 10 μm, gap 5.5 μm each side
Boundary box
2 mm × 2 mm
Table grid
R ∈ … μm  ·  D ∈ … μm  ·  s ∈ … μm
Interpolation
Trilinear (linear in R, D, s independently)
ⓘ Accuracy note: Capacitances from this calculator are more likely to be underestimated than overestimated, typically by ~20 %. Two reasons: (1) the BEM solves for charge distribution in 2D rather than full 3D field lines — mathematically equivalent only under idealised assumptions; (2) for large islands or wide centre-to-centre distances the feedline segment can no longer be treated as infinitely long, reducing the effective coupling captured by the simulation.
⚠ Parameter combination is outside or near the edge of the BEM grid — result involves extrapolation and may be less accurate.
